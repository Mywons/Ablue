# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary: procurement / sourcing at automotive Tier-1s and OEMs.** Buyers
at Dana, Eaton, ZF, Meritor and equivalent global suppliers, often working
through a Brazilian sourcing office. They land here while evaluating three
to five forging suppliers during a sourcing event, usually under time
pressure, and they need to decide within minutes whether Metalcoop is
worth an RFQ or a plant visit. They also frequently need to forward a link
to a manager or quality lead as evidence that the supplier is credible.

A buyer's questions, in order of how fast they need to be answered:
1. What can you forge — sizes, materials, tolerances?
2. Are you IATF 16949 certified, and can I see proof?
3. Who else buys from you?
4. Where are you, and how do I get a quote?

**Secondary: process and part engineers** at the same accounts, evaluating
whether Metalcoop's cold and warm forging capabilities fit a specific
component. They want technical depth: the cold-vs-warm forging tradeoff,
fibragem orientada, post-forging operations, lot traceability.

**Tertiary: prospective cooperados** in the Salto/SP region applying via
Trabalhe Conosco. Important but does not drive the design language;
served by a dedicated section, not by the main brand register.

Out of scope as a primary audience: cooperative-movement researchers,
labor unions, ISO/IATF auditors. Their needs are met as a side effect of
the procurement-first design, not by tailoring to them.

## Product Purpose

The site is Metalcoop's outward-facing supplier credentials surface. Its
job is to convert qualified procurement and engineering visits into RFQs
and plant visits, while giving existing customers a current, IATF-credible
reference they can show internally.

Success looks like:
- A buyer at a Tier-1 can get from landing to "send the RFQ" in five
  minutes, without scrolling past marketing language.
- An engineer can find the technical answer they came for (forging mode,
  process steps, what Metalcoop will and will not run) without asking.
- The site looks like Metalcoop, not like a Brazilian industrial template.
- Forms work, certifications are visible, and the cooperative model is
  legible without being the headline.

## Brand Personality

**Forged. Dimensional. Plural.**

- **Forged** is process as identity. The forge is the subject, not a
  background image. Heat, mass, the press in motion, the geometry of the
  die. Not a metaphor; the literal thing Metalcoop does for a living.
- **Dimensional** is the language of the spec sheet. Tolerance numbers,
  lot codes, °C, Ø, ±, IATF clauses. Specifications are content, not
  decoration. Numbers carry the typography.
- **Plural** is the cooperative voice. *We* and *the cooperados*, not *I*
  and not the absent third-person of corporate copy. The cooperative
  model is the byline and the colophon, not the hero.

The personality is **plain-spoken observation of a violent process**.
Calm, patient, technical voice describing real heat and real mass. The
calm comes from confidence in the work, not from sterile whitespace.
*"850 °C. Tolerance ±0.05. Lot 2026-A2. Produzido pelos cooperados da
Metalcoop."* That sentence is the brand.

Tone rules that follow from the trio:
- Numbers and units appear inline in body copy, not only in tables.
- The cooperative is mentioned plainly, never theatrically. No
  solidarity-fist iconography, no "we believe in workers" mission slabs.
- The forge process is described, not advertised. *Forjamento a frio
  reduz operações posteriores de usinagem* beats *qualidade superior*.
- Confidence comes from specificity. Vague claims ("alta qualidade")
  are weaker than measured ones ("±0.05 mm em produção contínua").
- Portuguese is the native voice. English is a faithful translation, not
  a watered-down marketing rewrite.

## Anti-references

Five lanes the redesign must not retreat into.

**1. Generic emerald-green corporate (the current Metalcoop site).**
Tailwind-emerald palette as section wash, gradient buttons, Inter, soft
drop shadows, hover-translate(-4px) cards, identical icon-circle-card
grids, ISO badges floating as decoration. Hero carousel followed by
three feature cards followed by contact form. The default Brazilian
web-agency layout, and the default AI-template skin.

Brand-color constraint: the locked Metalcoop greens (`#059669`,
`#047857`) and white (`#ffffff`) are kept as part of the brand, but
their *role* changes. Walking away means: brand emerald appears only
as a *signature* on the logo, certification marks (ISO 9001, IATF
16949), the *Produzido pelos cooperados* colophon, and focus rings.
No green washes, no green CTA fills, no green section backgrounds.
A non-emerald forge-warm secondary (cast-iron + ember-orange + paper)
carries the rest of the visual chassis. Plus: no gradient buttons,
no card grids, no Inter, no auto-advancing carousel hero, no
soft-shadow cards.

**2. The Brazilian-industrial-template lane.** Schulz, Brasilforjas, Aços
Villares, Forjas Taurus and the broader cohort of Brazilian forge sites:
blue-or-green corporate header, stock industrial photography, generic
*Quem Somos / Produtos / Contato* nav, ISO badges as wallpaper. Walking
away means: a buyer comparing five Brazilian forge sites should remember
which one was Metalcoop.

**3. Tier-1 clean-spec sterility (Sandvik Coromant, Bosch, GKN, ZF
technical site).** White space as quality, drafting-blue accent,
restrained typography, calm. This is the second-order reflex — the
obviously sophisticated answer for a procurement audience. Walking away
means: commit to forge-lane materiality (heat, soot, the weight of the
billet) and do not retreat into a clean-spec safety net to look serious.
Forged-dimensional-plural ≠ Sandvik-blue.

**4. Cooperative-movement / solidarity-poster aesthetic.** Red-and-black
print, hand-set type, MST publication feel, documentary photography of
workers organized as a collective subject, raised-fist iconography,
plural-civic art direction. The cooperative origin is part of the story.
The visual lane is the forge, not the movement. Walking away means: the
cooperative shows up in voice (*we*, *cooperados*) and in colophon, not
as a graphic identity.

**5. Marketing-agency hero-carousel-then-cards-then-form.** Auto-advancing
banner with vague headlines, three identical feature cards, two CTA
buttons, contact form, dark footer. The structural cliché of the current
site. Walking away means: every page finds its own structure, the home
page in particular. No carousel hero. No three-card section.

## Design Principles

**1. The forge is the surface, not the background.**
Real Metalcoop fachada photos, real parts, real cooperados when people
appear. No abstract "industry" iconography, no stock images of gears /
handshakes / globes / blueprints. If a section can't be illustrated with
real Metalcoop material, it is illustrated with type.

**2. Specifications are the typography.**
Tolerance numbers, °C, Ø, ±, IATF 16949 clauses, lot codes, fios e barras
diameters set the typographic tone. They live inline in body copy, not
quarantined inside tables. Headlines borrow their voice from the spec
sheet, not from the marketing brief.

**3. Procurement first; cooperative model as signature.**
Every page answers a buyer's question within five seconds: capability,
certification, customers, contact. The cooperative model appears as a
plain credit (*Produzido pelos cooperados da Metalcoop*), not as a hero
panel. The work proves the model; the model does not need to argue for
itself.

**4. Calm voice, hot subject.**
The personality is patient and dimensional, but the process is violent
(heat, force, deformation). The voice does not perform the violence and
does not sterilize it. It describes it plainly, the way a forge supervisor
would describe it to another supervisor. No marketing prose layer. No
exclamation points.

**5. Vary structure per page, not just content.**
The five pages should not share a structural template that gets refilled
with different copy. The home page is not a longer empresa page. The
produtos page is built around process and part data, not generic feature
cards. Repeating layouts read as a CMS theme, which is the opposite of
forged.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.2 AA** as the floor, including the new touch-target rule
  (24×24 minimum, 44×44 preferred). Procurement departments at global
  Tier-1s increasingly run accessibility scans on supplier sites for
  ESG and DE&I compliance; failing AA is a sourcing risk.
- **`prefers-reduced-motion`** honored on every animation, not just the
  slider. Users with vestibular sensitivity should see a static site.
- **Bilingual content (PT primary, EN faithful translation)** wired
  through the `lang` attribute on `<html>` and `<section>` boundaries
  so screen readers switch voice correctly. The current half-broken
  `data-i18n` machinery must be replaced or removed.
- **Low-bandwidth tolerance.** Industrial environments, sourcing offices
  on tethered laptops, and pre-RFQ phone-screen comparisons may all
  happen on slow links. The site should land usable on 3G and stay
  legible without webfonts or images loaded.
- **Print-friendly capabilities + certifications page.** A buyer
  forwarding the page to a manager should be able to print the
  capability statement and the certification list without losing layout
  or context.
- **No motion as information.** Status, hierarchy, and meaning never
  depend on color or movement alone.
